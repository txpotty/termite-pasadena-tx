Implementing Effective Treatment Methods

Pasadena Termite Pro employs a variety of proven treatment methods, selecting the most appropriate one based on your specific situation. Our goal is to eliminate the existing termite colony and create a protective barrier around your property.

Liquid Termiticide Applications

One common and highly effective method is the application of liquid termiticides. These products are applied to the soil around the foundation of your home, creating a treated zone that termites cannot penetrate. As termites attempt to cross this barrier, they are exposed to the termiticide, which they can also transfer to the rest of the colony, leading to a domino effect of elimination. Termite Baiting Systems

Another effective approach is the use of termite baiting systems. These systems involve strategically placing bait stations around your property. The bait contains a slow-acting termiticide that termites consume and then carry back to their colony, eventually eliminating the entire colony. Baiting systems are often a good option for properties with sensitive environments or for ongoing monitoring and prevention.

In some cases, direct treatment of infested wood might be necessary. This involves applying specialized products directly to areas of wood where termites are actively feeding. These treatments can kill termites within the wood and prevent future infestations in those areas. Subterranean Termite Infestations

Subterranean termites are the most prevalent type of termite in the Houston, TX area. They live in underground colonies and build mud tubes to travel from the soil to their food source – the cellulose found in wood. These mud tubes are a clear indicator of their presence and activity. Finding mud tubes on your foundation walls, crawl spaces, or even inside your home is a strong signal that you have a subterranean termite problem that requires professional intervention. Our team is highly experienced in identifying subterranean termite colonies and implementing targeted treatments using liquid termiticides or baiting systems to eliminate these destructive pests and protect your home's structure. Drywood Termite Damage

Unlike subterranean termites, drywood termites can infest wood that is not in contact with the soil. Signs of Termite Activity You Shouldn't Ignore

Being proactive in identifying potential termite issues can save you significant costs and headaches down the line. Here are some common signs that indicate you might have a termite problem and should contact a professional immediately:

Discarded Termite Wings

Often the first visible sign of a termite infestation is the presence of discarded wings. Termite swarmers, the reproductive alates, shed their wings after they emerge from the colony to mate and establish new colonies. Finding piles of clear, delicate wings near windowsills, doorways, or light fixtures is a strong indication of nearby termite activity. These swarms typically occur during warmer months, and seeing them is a clear call to action. Don't delay contacting us if you notice discarded wings.

Mud Tubes on Walls or Foundation

Subterranean termites build protective mud tubes to travel between their underground colony and their food source. These tubes are typically about the width of a pencil and can be found on foundation walls, concrete slabs, support beams, or even interior walls. They serve as enclosed pathways that protect termites from predators and drying out. The presence of mud tubes is a definitive sign of a subterranean termite infestation and should be addressed promptly by a professional termite control service. Our team is adept at locating and identifying these mud tubes, understanding their importance as indicators of active termite colonies and their pathways to accessing your property's wooden elements. We don't just remove the tubes; we trace them back to the source to ensure that the treatment effectively targets the entire colony. The integrity of these mud tubes is crucial for the survival of subterranean termites, and their presence signifies a thriving colony actively causing potential damage to your property. We understand the urgency that comes with discovering mud tubes and prioritize swift assessment and treatment to minimize further structural damage. Our technicians are trained to distinguish between active and inactive mud tubes, but even inactive tubes can indicate a past infestation and the potential for re-infestation, warranting a thorough inspection. The material used to construct these tubes, typically soil particles mixed with termite saliva and feces, is distinctive and easily recognizable to our experienced professionals. By understanding the behavioral patterns associated with mud tube construction and utilization, we can more effectively target our treatment efforts and interrupt the termite's foraging activities. We also advise our clients on how to minimize factors that encourage mud tube construction, such as reducing soil contact with wooden elements and maintaining a dry perimeter around the foundation. Our comprehensive approach ensures that we address the immediate issue of mud tubes while also implementing strategies for long-term prevention of subterranean termite activity on your property.
